CONIGLIO VERDE

Green Rabbit was born from a research on the contamination between languages, art and technology.
The name is inspired by Eduardo Kacâ€™s Alba project and his research on new forms of aesthetic and poetic experience.

Green Rabbit is a multimedia exhibition space and a research laboratory on topics such as augmented realities, AI, hybridization and sustainability.

Green Rabbit does not like to waste, and reuses everything he can when he can, be it people, things, time or ideas, because he believes that

everything can be transformed.


DATA

From the point of view of the Green Rabbit it is clear that data today takes on a new and complex meaning.
The data represents the digital and material fabric of our present, whispering shreds of premonitions as the oracles once did.

Data represents an extension of ourselves that reveals and hides, at the same time, our lives, shaping their traits in ways still unknown to us.

CINEMATIC

Green Rabbit, sitting comfortably in the cinema chair, reflected on how a film could be confused with a dream.

That cinema that made entire generations dream has multiplied and fragmented spreading on screens of all kinds to project itself on the surfaces of architectural structures.

Green Rabbit believes that cinematography is still a form of communication and art that has not exhausted its strength, but is also attracted by its contemporary declinations such as immersive cinematic experiences.

WRITING

Green Rabbit treasures the phrase of a narrator of strange stories.

This narrator said that not all stories should have the shape of a house, the one we can most easily imagine, with a roof, windows, a door and a beautiful garden.

Some stories can take the form of elephants, coffee cups, planetary systems and who knows what else.

Green Rabbit knows that words can create new worlds.

STATIC

Time impresses on sensitive surfaces through light. The images slide, but some of them get caught, they stick to the look.
Green Rabbit experiments with static images created with AI, generative algorithms, digital photography.

The light of the future, the shadow of the past. If time turns on itself, it may be that desire and nostalgia turn out to be, after all, the same thing.
