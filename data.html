H.E.Arts

H.E.Arts stands for High Emotional Arts, a device based on the "Geneva Wheel" and designed to record the emotional journey of a visitor to a museum, art gallery or other types of artistic enjoyment.

The visitor can select the type of emotion he feels to describe his experience, and then see it translated into a personalized and creative data visualization.

The prototype on this page allows the user to record their emotions starting from a selection of works from the 900 Museum of Milan.

The experience takes place in this way. To start, during the visit, take your time, look at the works of art and "let yourself be heard".
Pay attention to your emotions in relation to what works could mean.

"What matters is what you try."

In our case you will find the selected works in the app interface.

In the interface you will find a gallery and a list of emotions that you can select. Emotions are grouped into four key areas.

Choose one, two or up to three to describe the nuances of your emotions.
After making the first choice drag the image to go to the next work and so on to the last one.

At the end of the gallery select the "result" button and you can see how your personal data create a unique work, the result of your personal experience.

At the end of the data collection, which you can end at any time, you can check the result.

A first visualization is designed to create a space to explore H.E.Arts_01.

A second visualization creates a unique object, inspired by one of the artists in the museum H.E.Arts_02.

If you like you can use our contacts to tell us about your experience.
