H.E.Arts

H.E.Arts stands for High Emotional Arts, a device based on the "Geneva Wheel" and designed to record the emotional journey of a visitor to a museum, gallery or other artistic experience.
The visitor can select the type of emotion he feels to describe his experience which results in a personal and creative data visualization.
The first prototype on this page sees the user recording their emotions on a selection of works from the Museum of 900 Milan.

The experience takes place in this way. To start, during the visit, take your time, look at the paintings and "let yourself be heard".
Pay more attention to your emotions than to what paintings should mean.
"What matters is what you feel."
In our case you will find the selected works in the app interface.

In the interface you will find a gallery and a list of emotions that you can select. The emotions are grouped into four fundamental areas and each basic color draws them: yellow/ cheerful magenta/ sensitive blue/ sad green/ annoying.
Choose one, two or up to three to describe the nuances of your emotions.
After making the first choice, swipe the image with your finger to go to the next work and so on until the end.
At this point select the "result" button and you can see how your personal data create a unique work of your personal experience.

At the end of the data collection, which you can end at any time, you can check the result.
A first visualization is designed for young audiences, h.e.arts.01, while a second display is imagined an adult audience, and is inspired by one of the artists in the museum, guess which? h.e.arts_02

This prototype is open to many evolutions, if you like you can use our contacts to let us know your opinion.
